## 30-Second Pitch

"This is a travel agency operator tool.

Instead of manually building client offers from screenshots, your operator uploads flight and hotel screenshots. The system extracts the key details, enriches hotel options with real images, calculates the final client price, and generates a premium PDF offer with a WhatsApp QR for immediate client follow-up.

The goal is not to replace the agent. The goal is to make the agent faster and make every offer look premium."

## 3-Minute Demo Script

1. Open admin.

Say:
"This is the operator workspace. It is not client-facing."

2. Show Create Offer.

Say:
"The agent enters the client name, destination, dates, guests, and internal pricing rules."

3. Upload flight screenshot.

Say:
"The system reads the flight screenshot and turns it into a structured flight card."

4. Upload hotel screenshot.

Say:
"The system reads the hotel screenshot, extracts hotel details and price, then enriches it with a real hotel image."

5. Show dynamic builder.

Say:
"The operator can keep multiple flights and multiple hotel options. The selected hotel drives the final client price."

6. Save offer.

Say:
"Internal pricing stays inside the admin. The client only sees the final offer."

7. Open client offer.

Say:
"This is what the client receives: a premium travel proposal, not an admin export."

8. Open PDF.

Say:
"The same offer becomes a PDF suitable for WhatsApp, email, or printing."

9. Show WhatsApp QR.

Say:
"The client can scan this QR and immediately start a WhatsApp conversation with the agency."

Close:
"The whole flow starts from screenshots the agency already uses today."

## Agency Discovery Questions

Ask:

- How do you currently create client offers?
- How long does one offer take from request to send?
- Do agents copy data from screenshots manually?
- Do you send offers by WhatsApp, email, PDF, or all three?
- Do you compare multiple hotel options for the same client?
- Where do mistakes usually happen?
- What makes a client say yes faster?
- What would make this unusable in your current workflow?

Listen for:

- manual copy/paste pain
- price mistakes
- slow offer turnaround
- inconsistent PDF quality
- WhatsApp-first communication
- hotel comparison needs
- operator training friction

## Beta Fit Criteria

Good beta agency:

- creates custom offers manually
- uses screenshots from airlines / Booking / suppliers
- communicates heavily through WhatsApp
- sends PDFs or visual proposals
- has at least one operator who repeats this workflow weekly
- cares about presentation quality

Poor beta fit:

- only sells fixed package tours
- does not create custom offers
- does not use WhatsApp with clients
- needs public self-serve booking immediately
- expects full accounting / CRM / billing now

## Objection Handling

Objection:
"Can it make mistakes?"

Answer:
"Yes, screenshots can be messy. That is why the system keeps the operator in control. AI extracts the draft, but the agent reviews and edits before sending."

Objection:
"Is this replacing our agents?"

Answer:
"No. It removes repetitive formatting and copy/paste work. The agent still chooses the offer, checks conditions, and owns the client relationship."

Objection:
"Can clients book directly?"

Answer:
"Not in the beta. The current goal is faster premium proposals and WhatsApp conversion. Direct booking can come later if the workflow proves useful."

Objection:
"Can it use our branding?"

Answer:
"Yes, branding is a natural next step after beta validation. For now we are validating the core offer workflow."

Objection:
"What about payments?"

Answer:
"Billing and payments are intentionally not part of this beta. The first validation is whether the agency can create better offers faster."

## Beta Call Structure

Before call:

- open production admin
- open one client offer
- open one generated PDF
- prepare two screenshots
- confirm Railway production is online
- confirm QR opens WhatsApp

During call:

1. Ask current workflow questions.
2. Show the finished PDF first.
3. Show how it was generated from screenshots.
4. Ask where this fits in their daily workflow.
5. Ask what would block adoption.
6. Ask if they want to test with real screenshots.

After call:

- write down exact objections
- write down requested changes
- mark whether the request is workflow-critical or feature creep
- do not implement immediately unless it blocks beta usage
